The founder of Python is Guido van Rossum, a Dutch programmer born on January 31, 1956, who created the language to make programming more intuitive, readable, and enjoyable. He served as Python's project leader for nearly three decades and is universally recognized as one of the most influential figures in modern computer science.🚀 The Birth of PythonThe Origin: Van Rossum began implementing Python in December 1989 as a holiday hobby project. He wanted to create an interpreter for a new scripting language that improved upon ABC, a language he previously worked on at his job.The Launch: He published the first official open-source version (Python 0.9.0) in February 1991.The Name: The language is not named after the snake. Van Rossum was a big fan of the British comedy series Monty Python's Flying Circus and wanted a name that was short, unique, and slightly mysterious.👑 The "Benevolent Dictator for Life" (BDFL)For 27 years, the Python community affectionately titled Van Rossum the "Benevolent Dictator for Life" (BDFL). This meant he held the final say on major design choices and the direction of the language. He officially stepped down from his BDFL role in July 2018 to hand leadership over to a community-elected steering council. He now retains the title "BDFL-emeritus".🎓 Background and CareerEducation: He grew up in the Netherlands and earned a Master’s Degree in Mathematics and Computer Science from the University of Amsterdam in 1982.Early Work: He developed Python while working at the Centrum Wiskunde & Informatica (CWI), a prominent Dutch research institute.Big Tech Roles: Van Rossum moved to the United States in 1995. He later worked as a Senior Staff Engineer at Google (2005–2012) and a Principal Engineer at Dropbox (2013–2019).Current Status: After a very brief retirement, he joined Microsoft in 2020, where he serves as a Distinguished Engineer.
